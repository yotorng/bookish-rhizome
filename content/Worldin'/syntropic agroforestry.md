## Stage 3 - Abundance system
![[Abundance system 2.png|400]]
*treelines with a diversity of trees and types of plants!*
abundance system in the sense where it creates and gives more

- bear fruits durian coconut
- animals - biggest ones come frm these

- What are some gifts we(humans) have?
	- hands
	- organisation
- a human belonging to a forest system is to apply these gifts.
- will our human intervention do better than just leaving the forest by itself? if yes, continue to intervene meaningfully, but if we aren't, leave and let the forest do its own thing. 
- mombasa grass replaces lalang - species replacement can be done, this time by humans, to maximise photosynthesis 
- concrete energy by mulching for the treelines
	- creating leadership lines of regeneration
	- treelines are the next generation
- "Water can be planted" - Ernst Götsch (agua se sth) - no need for irrigation!
	- https://vimeo.com/287503052
- cutting grass and feed to the parent 
- the fastest way to start is to infect - 1 species first, but you can intentionally start to grow it further
- the idea of natural succession - happens in time and space ; monoculture - 2 dimensions (x/y axis)
- in syntropic agroforestry: there are 5 different dimensions in which this takes place:
	1. x - breadth
	2. y - length
	3. height - stratification
		- Simplified to 4 stratas: 
			- emergent(love sun, e.g. durian coconut etc)
			- canopy(high, e.g. jackfruit rambutan)
			- medium(shade loving, e.g. lime, mangosteen, banana)
			- low trees(absolutely love shade, e.g. cacao, pineapple, coffee, ginger)
		- learn ecophysiology 
	4. time - on different scales
		- systemic scale from pioneer to accumulation to abundance 
		- the scale of life cycles: 
			- 3-5 years are considered short 
			- the shorter life cycles are known as *placentas* - e.g. papaya are placenta-ing the cacaos, short life cycle trees grow faster and mother the longer life cycle trees, creating conditions for the next tree eg through tending to soil, mulching, etc
	5. diversity
		- takes a lot of energy and grow super fast as possible vs **resilience** where diverse immune systems for the forest is created, create infinite relationships and interactions with diversity

*layered planting*

- knowledge based and process based as opposed to input/output based
- the dynamics of interaction w farm creates the conditions
- 5% planting, 95% management 
	- prune support trees, concentrating energy
- Always asking: "How can I serve the land's dream to be a forest."
- dont treat any tree as an individual, but an organ in an organism
	- if the cacao is an organ, what equivalent macroorganism are you creating to allow the cacao to thrive
- 20% forests are always in disturbance
	- fire is one example (natural disturbance)
	- orang asli practice fire (5000 acre) - fire is there when you have the forest but not when you are contained to 1 acre (quickly diminish) , cannot repeat it  
	- we(humans) are doing the same role, but non-fire disturbance
	- instead - pulsing the system - a fresh pulse for the next evolution, reset the system and look for a new pulse
- maximise life in all ways possible
- pests are fire alarm - an indicator of how the tree is doing
- major pruning to introduce sunlight
- strata pruning - crown lifting, keeping it low etc etc
- the joy of fulfilling your function to the macroorganism
	- tuning your inner joy towards the land 
	- naturally fulfill and maximise 
	- trusting your inner joy - actors of a certain design dont even know their design function
	- alludes to wild organisms and their roles in different ecosystems:
		- wild boar digging hole - creating reservoir, not actively thinking about this function, just leaning into its inner, subconscious joy
		- same as a monkey breaking a branch, not about mulching!
	- letting the inner joy lead you
