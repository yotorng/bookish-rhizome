Book by Siang Lu

Back with another metafictional read! This time prompted by Siang Lu’s visit to this year’s Singapore Writers Festival, and also the sheer coincidence that Ghost Cities has been lying around unread on my shelf for a year now...  
  
Ghost Cities consists of two interwoven storylines that form a sprawling satirical tapestry, blurring the mythical and the modern. I won’t attempt to summarise the plot because there’s So Much...Did I have fun? Yes. 🙂‍↕️Did I fully comprehend it? Probably not! 🤠  
  
There’s a playful levity to Ghost Cities in both plot and form. Jokes are set up in absurd manners, and there’s a deep commitment to these bits (with wild payoffs...thinking about Baby Bao’s Baby). The story moves deftly through an array of vehicles: epic poems, allegorical fables, tongue-in-cheek public announcements, but also through occasionally tender depictions of connection and care.  
  
Beneath its hilarious demeanour, Ghost Cities is incredibly ambitious in its scope. It reaches for big themes of:  
🌾how life and art endures amidst totalitarianism,  
🎭the prosaic performance of the self amidst unstable social realities,  
🏙️cities, its inhabitants and its stories as living, breathing palimpsests, constantly being made and remade  
  
“It is hard to know anymore, if we are still playing with words or if we are saying real things.” ok Siang/Xiang Lu,,,,I see you,,!! 😗✋A dense and discombobulating read, but I’ve yet to stop thinking about it.  
  
🍃Ending my review with some things that still linger in my mind, in no particular order:  
- Port Man Tou and its citizen-actors. Department of Verisimilitude. Canton of our Subconscious Choreography. Canton of Print to PDF. Canton of Time’s Uncertain Arrow. etc etc  
- #\BadChinese
- The granny witnessing her own funeral procession  
- The miniature village created by the Imperial Artisan, so loyal in its depiction of the village aging over time that it becomes dismissed as a work of an amateur  
- Chickens 🐓 & Imperial Swine 🐷
