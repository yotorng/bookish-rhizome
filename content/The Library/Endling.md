Book by Maria Reva

Endling packs a punch💥Where do I even begin! It features:  
😔1 depressed snail scientist musing about futility as she goes around Ukraine rescuing endangered snails,  
🤬2 daughters of a missing angry feminist activist (who disappeared after years of protesting the Ukrainian mail-order bride industry),  
🚚😵a truckload of kidnapped Western men looking for love, and  
🐌a last-of-its-species snail called Lefty with its rare genetic mutation of a left-coiling shell…  
🇺🇦all this set amidst the backdrop of Russia’s invasion of Ukraine.  
  
With this absurd laundry list of characters and plot points, Endling feels like a book that, on paper, shouldn’t have worked as well as it did. But wow did I enjoy this absolute ride of a book,, In an era where truth often feels stranger than fiction, I loved the way Maria Reva’s voice pierces through, blurring the story’s boundaries with reality. She deftly harnesses dark humour amidst devastation, and asserts stories as a form of meaning-making amidst troubled times.  
  
Reva doesn’t just carve out characters from thin air. There are traces of real humans and their lived experiences in the face of war. I’m left thinking about  
the grandfather who stubbornly refuses to leave Ukraine, his steel door, and his bowl of soup 🥣 I hope he’s doing okay.
