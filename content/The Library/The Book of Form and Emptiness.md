Book by Ruth Ozeki

>"A book must start somewhere. One brave letter must volunteer to go first, laying itself on the line in an act of faith, from which a word takes heart and follows, drawing a sentence into its wake. From there, a paragraph amasses, and soon a page, and the book is on its way, finding a voice, calling itself into being."

After the death of his jazz musician father, Benny Oh starts hearing voices—the inner worlds of objects around him. As his grief-stricken mother Annabelle develops a hoarding problem at home, the voices become excruciatingly overwhelming. Until one day, Benny discovers the Library where the voices are hushed. From there, he draws solace from books, unlikely companions, and his very own Book. 

Finally finished my second Ruth Ozeki title! Equal parts heartwrenching and heartwarming, this book is a humble homage to enduring power of stories, to the tales we tell ourselves to make sense of reality. Ascribing voices to various objects, Ozeki illuminates their curious and arcane lives. 

>"Human language is a clumsy tool. People have such a hard time understanding each other, so how can you even begin to imagine the subjectivities of animals and insects and plants, never mind pebbles and sand? Bound as you are by your senses—so blunt and yet so beautiful—it's impossible for you to imagine that the myriad beings you dismiss as insentient might have inner lives, too. Books are in an odd position, caught halfway in between. We are sensible, if not sentient. We are semi-living."

And yet—there is nothing clumsy about the way Ozeki wields language!! I remain so in awe of her way with words, her ability to render messy emotions and lives tenderly and sensitively. The lives of Benny and Annabelle are discordant and absurd, their grief constantly unfurling and ravaging, their love tenuous yet striving. There is something incredibly enchanting about how Ozeki elevates the mundane, capturing the experience of being. 

Ultimately, A Book of Form and Emptiness spins a glorious tale of our worldly attachment to material possessions, and our otherworldly dispositions towards connection, grief and love. 🪟🎺🐣
