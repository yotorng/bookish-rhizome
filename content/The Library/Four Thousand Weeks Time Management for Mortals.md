Book by Oliver Burkeman

2024's first book falls very nicely(and all too revealing🤥) trope of renewal, reflection and 
a dash of panic amidst transitory szns!!!


The title is actually indicative of the average lifespan of humans- just a measly 4 thousand weeks(yup..u read that right..🤣).

Honestly I can summarise the book in just 4 words: "fk it we ball!" Burkeman argues that ironically, embracing our finite lifespan could be potentially freeing. The argument was clear from the get-go, and various chunks of the book felt a tad repetitive to me. Here are my distillations ⚗️👩‍🔬:
- most of us invest a lot of energy, one way or another, in trying to avoid fully experiencing the reality in which we find ourselves. 
- missing out is what makes our choices meaningful in the first place. 
- if you plan to spend some of your four thousand weeks doing what matters most to you, then at some point you are just going to have to start doing it...limit your work in progress!!
- time as a network good(ok this was my favourite part of the book) !! What kind of freedom do we really want when it comes to time? First there's the culturally celebrated goal of individual time sovereignty—the freedom to set your own schedule, to make ur choices, to be free from other people's intrusions into ur precious 4 thousand weeks. But there's also the profound sense of meaning that comes from being willing to fall in with the rhythms of the rest of the world, to be free to engage in all the worthwhile collaborative endeavours that require at least some sacrifice of your sole control over what you do and when (think about collective organising, or carving out time to spend with your loved ones). I really like his argument that the value of time isn't just measured through the quantity that you have or how you instrumentally use it, but rather how time flows through you and your network, whether you're in sync with your environment/community/surroundings

Here's to stepping more fully into the condition of being a very.. bounded and limited human existence 🤠
