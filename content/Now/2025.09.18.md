## notes from sdw's design futures forum 
I attended my first design futures forum yesterday, felt ambivalent about it for the most part. the broad overarching themes of care, sustainability and emerging tech felt way too expansive and loose to reveal meaningful intersections, and I found myself enjoying the focused sharing sessions more. 

the world we imagine can be one that we inhabit

- dont view nature & tech as a binary
- interacting with nature (in the context of biomaterial) viewing the interaction as natural beings, not as products
	- not just manipulating them to shape a design outcome, but seeing them as agents & respecting them as intelligent beings
- design as a method to explore and invite options
- inviting more-than-human beings as collaborators in these design endeavours but to what end? (bamboo, fungi, shifting human focus on various form of nature, melding and shaping that to our whims)
- smallholder farms being replaced by industrial farming - how do u work w smallholder, in designing local, place-based solutions
- the collapsing of scales when it comes to design
	- product design of iphone leading to terraforming landscapes on the other side of the world (to extract rare metals)
	- you cant silo design
- holding deep engagements with extremity
- failure of games - as a way to represent system collapse, but also the ripe arena where renewal and possibilities can emerge
### ways of worlding - alice bucknell[^1]
- https://alicebucknell.com/
- video games as affective interfaces, mediating between human and non-humans
- situated in software 
- using speculation as a critical tool, fiction as a way to playtest worlds, rs, planets
- video games as a platform to rehearse empathy - empathy engine
	- spawning in a new world when you start a game -> embracing uncertainty, the void of knowledge, being open n permeable to the world around as a precondition of play
- games as machines for getting lost on purpose
- resources
	- rambunctious games: an environmental game manifesto 
	- alenda chang playing nature (book)
- creating games that are capable of conjuring different forms of environments, as opposed to landscapes being rendered inert in some games (following an economic imperative for first person shooters, disaster type of violent games)
- what if we imagine the environment as the main player
- what can play - the potential of non-human players (kara stone) (journal article)
	flower - where u play as wind, embodying an elemental, non-human entity, where the boundary between you as a subject and environment is permeable 
- using procedural generation - ai complicating the narrative opportunities in vid games, decentering the human (subjects hving a set level of agency, npc learn frm ur actions and react progressively, ai agents as a device for ecological storytelling that decenters the human) 
	- alluvials (game by alice) - 1 level playing as la river, pack of wolves, joshua tree(symbions), wildfire(walking simulator, world as agent and presenter of stories ; fire as an agent ; neg view in relation to property n ownership)
- video games as a tool for pulling us out of our common, default narratives, opening up spaces of possibilities, diff ways of seeing;relating to;interfacing with the world
- nightcrawlers
	- nocturnal pollinators - bats as a keystone species that prop up ecosystems
	- nocturnal flowers
	- using gameplay to represent the senses of smell & sonar (decentering the sense of sight in an overwhelmingly visual world) - echolocation 
	- call & response game of sound; an ode to interspecies communication
- earth engine
	- using real-time place-based data & climate simulation projections
	- controls you as a player, shaping the world based on ur behaviours
	- history of radio telescope & remote sensing (satellites as a way to sense our worlds in higher resolution, what if the world could use those same, very systems to sense itself further)
	- CLOUDS AS MODEL ANARCHISTS - difficult in predicting them, throwing all ur geoengineering predictive models into askew ; scaleless beings
