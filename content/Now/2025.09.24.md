# How to Dream a Monster

^8db5ac

attended the 'A Bridge to the Classics' NLB talk by Dr Michelle Chiang after work[^1]! 

[[Frankenstein]]
- The talk is not about the creation of life as a hubristic act
	- not a question of ***whether or not to build, but what to do after we 'succeed'***
- the poignant act of frankenstein fleeing after the creation
- Who becomes more monstrous: the creation or creator?
- Context that Mary Shelley was in: 1818 Romantic science and ambition
	- height of Romanticism (hitting against the advancement of science), at the time of Galvinism
	- permeable boundaries between life and non-life
	- anticipation of today's transhumanist ambitions to rewrite biology
	- using science to lead the way of /the world/
- Transhumanism
	- why accept human, biological limitations when we can transcend them
	- to transhumanists - the human body as a problem to be solved
	- morphological freedom (reshaping human form entirely) - e.g. nanobots ; advocacy for the right the modify our bodies any way we want, e.g. implanting ourselves with chips to expand or augment capabilities; break limits
	- ai-integrated cognition
	- longevity escape velocity - reduce ageing
	- Frankenstein as the proto-transhumanist 
- approach this idea by thinking about care ethics 
	- focus not on code of conducts, moral frameworks etc 
	- instead: through **relationality & affect** -> doing right/wrong among people
	- ambition untempered by **care** as compared to ethics
	- the monster is monstrous but not by nature, it is monstrous by neglect 
	- body parts initially chosen for beauty, pieced together 
	- *"i was benevolent and good; misery made me a fiend"*
	- monstrosity inflicted **upon** the monster
- precisely because difficult to take responsibility (to an outcome that might be not yet known)
- relationality of the 'other' : the subjugation, the marginalised, care ethics wants us to reframe this narrative about the other, as a subject in need of care, the subject is innately not understandable
- [[more-than-human|posthumanism]]
	- about tempering the ambitions of transhumanisms
	- moderation
	- rethink human mastery, live with our monsters, think about our responsibility 
	- donna haraway, [**rosi braidotti**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosi_Braidotti), n katherine hayles 
	- humans r entangled w machines, w one another, w non-humans, and the environment -> the flattening of human-centrism
	- dream of control is a fantasy
	- new forms of life, whether ai, engineered beings or hybrid ecologies, **demand relational ethics**, not domination
	- not sentimental or nostalgic about this type of apocalyptical future
- ai - autopoiesis frm cell biology 
	- organic beings (living cells) has a capacity to self-style, self-organise, generate, and regenerate
	- Guattari (the rhizome guy) tried to establish a qualitative link between the human and non-human
	- https://footnotes2plato.substack.com/p/autopoietic-machines-and-schizogenic
	- ai having the capacity to organise information 
- are we rushing to create/innovate without building systems of care around what we create?
	- **innovation as inevitable; destinies as evitable**
	- building monsters as inevitable BUT becoming monstrous is not inevitable
	- ex-machina 
	- interfacing with the machine as a reflection of **who you are**
- the necessity for posthuman education
	- integrating science w humanities
	- grounded in relationality ; the flattening of hierarchy
- monstrous in the context of the novel
	- the monster is seen as a monster as a of result differing from our beauty/aesthetic standards ; uncanny valley 
	- monstrosity from a series of actions
